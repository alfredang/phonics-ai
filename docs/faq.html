Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Phonics AI

General Questions

What is Phonics AI?

Phonics AI is a free, interactive web application that helps students aged 9-15 learn English pronunciation through games, lessons, and AI-powered features. It covers all 44 English phonemes and uses a structured approach: Listen, Practice, Play, and Assess.

Is Phonics AI free to use?

Yes! Phonics AI is completely free. There are no hidden costs, subscriptions, or in-app purchases. It was created as an educational tool for learners everywhere.

What age group is Phonics AI designed for?

Phonics AI is designed for students aged 9-15. However, it can be useful for anyone learning English phonics, including adult English language learners who want to improve their pronunciation.

Do I need to download an app?

No download is needed! Phonics AI runs directly in your web browser. Just visit the website and start learning. It works on computers, tablets, and smartphones.

Account & Progress

Do I need to create an account?

Creating an account is recommended to save your progress, earn XP, and unlock achievements. However, you can explore the app without an account - you just won't be able to save your progress.

How do I create an account?

Click "Sign Up" or "Register" on the app's homepage. Enter your email address and create a password. That's it! You can also sign up with your Google account for faster access.

I forgot my password. What do I do?

On the login page, click "Forgot Password" and enter your email address. You'll receive an email with instructions to reset your password.

Is my progress saved automatically?

Yes! If you're logged into your account, your progress is automatically saved to the cloud. You can continue learning from any device by logging into your account.

Can I use Phonics AI on multiple devices?

Yes! Your progress syncs across all devices when you're logged in. Start a lesson on your computer and continue on your tablet without losing any progress.

Learning & Lessons

How are the lessons structured?

Every lesson follows a 4-phase structure:

  1. Listen - Hear the correct pronunciation with visual guides
  2. Practice - Try the sounds yourself with feedback
  3. Play - Reinforce learning through fun games
  4. Assess - Test your knowledge and earn stars
What are the 5 worlds?

The 5 worlds represent different stages of learning:

  • Letters Land - Individual letter sounds
  • Word City - Blending sounds into words
  • Sentence Street - Reading simple sentences
  • Paragraph Park - Connected text reading
  • Story Kingdom - Fluent reading skills
Can I skip lessons or worlds?

Yes! While we recommend following the progression, you can work on any unlocked content. As you complete lessons, you'll unlock new worlds and more advanced content.

How do I earn stars?

Stars are earned based on your assessment score:

  • 3 Stars: 90-100% correct
  • 2 Stars: 70-89% correct
  • 1 Star: 50-69% correct

You can retry lessons to improve your star rating!

What is XP and how do I earn it?

XP (Experience Points) is a measure of your progress. You earn XP by:

  • Completing lessons
  • Playing games
  • Achieving high scores
  • Completing daily quests
  • Maintaining streaks

Technical Questions

Which browsers are supported?

Phonics AI works best on modern browsers:

  • Google Chrome (recommended)
  • Safari
  • Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge

For the best experience with speech features, we recommend Chrome.

The audio isn't working. What should I do?

Try these troubleshooting steps:

  1. Check that your device volume is turned up
  2. Make sure the browser tab isn't muted
  3. Try using headphones
  4. Refresh the page
  5. Try a different browser
The voice recording feature isn't working.

Voice recording requires microphone access. Here's what to check:

  1. Click "Allow" when the browser asks for microphone permission
  2. Check your browser settings to ensure microphone access is enabled
  3. Make sure no other application is using the microphone
  4. Try using Chrome browser for best compatibility
The app is loading slowly.

Slow loading can be caused by:

  • Slow internet connection - try moving closer to your WiFi router
  • Too many browser tabs open - close unused tabs
  • Outdated browser - update to the latest version

Refreshing the page often helps!

Does it work offline?

Currently, Phonics AI requires an internet connection to work. Progress is saved to the cloud, so you need to be online to access your account and save your progress.

For Parents

Is Phonics AI safe for children?

Yes! Phonics AI is designed with child safety in mind:

  • No advertisements
  • No social features or chat
  • No external links to other websites
  • Educational content only
  • No collection of personal data beyond login information
How much time should my child spend on the app?

We recommend 10-20 minutes of practice per day. Consistent, shorter sessions are more effective than long, infrequent ones. The daily quest system is designed to encourage regular practice without excessive screen time.

How can I support my child's learning?

Here are some ways to help:

  • Sit with your child occasionally to see what they're learning
  • Ask them to teach you the sounds they've learned
  • Celebrate their achievements and XP milestones
  • Point out the sounds they've learned in everyday reading
  • Use headphones for better focus

For Teachers

Can I use Phonics AI in my classroom?

Absolutely! Phonics AI is designed to complement classroom instruction. You can use it for:

  • Learning stations during literacy rotations
  • Independent practice
  • Homework assignments
  • Intervention support
  • Whole-class instruction (projected on screen)
Is there a way to manage multiple student accounts?

Currently, each student needs their own individual account. There is no classroom management dashboard yet, but this is a feature we're considering for future updates.

Does the curriculum align with my phonics program?

Phonics AI covers all 44 English phonemes following a systematic phonics approach. The sequence moves from simple to complex (letter sounds to CVC words to sentences to paragraphs to fluent reading). Most standard phonics curricula follow a similar progression.
